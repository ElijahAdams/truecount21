BlackJack Rules

Objective

The goal of blackjack is to beat the dealer's hand without going over 21

How to Play

  • Each player is dealt two cards at the start of the round (including the dealer).
  • Player's cards are publicly displayed except for the dealer. The dealer only shows 1 card until their turn.
  • Each card has a value associated with it that is used to determine the total of the hand.
  • Kings, Queens, Jacks and Tens all have a value of 10.
  • 9 - 2 all have the value of what is displayed on the card. (example: 7 has a value of 7)
  • Aces are unique with a value of 1 or 11. It becomes whichever is best for the player's hand.
  • Example This hands total is 15.
  • Each player gets to take a turn to try and get as close to 21 or having 21 without going over.
    • If a player has an Ace with a card valuing 10 in their initial hand they have Blackjack and have won unless the dealer also has Blackjack.
  • During a players turn they can take a few actions. Hit, Stay, Split*, Double Down*. (All actions explained below)
  • After all players complete actions for their turn. The dealer gets a turn.
  • The dealer can only Hit or Stay. The dealer will hit anything below 17 and soft 17's
  • Once all players and the dealer complete their turns. Those with a value greater than the dealer but not over 21 win.
    • Hands over 21 lose and are called a Bust.
    • Players with an equal total to the dealer Push.
    • If all players bust or all have blackjack or all have combo of the two determining a definitive win or loss before the dealers turn then the dealer will not take a hit regardless of their hands total.
    • If the dealer has blackjack on the initial deal the round is over an winners will be determined immediately.
Player Actions
  • Hit: player receives another card from the Shoe
  • Stay: player has completed their turn on that hand
  • Split*: player must have the same card for booth initial cards to be able to split. When splitting the player's hand becomes two hands each hand will have one of the original split cards and get a new card from the shoe. The player then plays out both hands.
    • Split Aces only get one new card from the shoe. Split aces cannot be hit again.
  • DoubleDown*: player must have an initial hand totaling to 10 or 11. (This is not a rule across all casinos) When a player doubles down they increase their bet by equal or lesser value of the original bet and they only receive one hit card which completes their turn.
Things Worth Mentioning
  • There are 52 cards in a deck. 4 cards of each value. 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 7, 8, 9, 10, Jack, Queen, King, Ace
  • Most Casinos play BlackJack with 6 decks of cards so that is true for this platform

Glossory

  • Soft: A hand with an ace valued as 11 is called "soft", meaning that the hand will not bust by taking an additional card.
  • Shoe: A physical card holder utilized in Casinos to hold cards to pull from to deal to players.
  • Bust: When a players hand is over 21.
  • Push: When the players hand total is equal to the dealers hand total.
